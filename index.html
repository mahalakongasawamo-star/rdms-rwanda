About RDMS

In Rwanda, a concerning trend has emerged among dentists regarding the low rate of dental check-ups nationwide. Alarm bells have been ringing about the potential oral health risks facing the population, highlighting an urgent need for action.

Recognizing the critical importance of regular dental check-ups in identifying and addressing dental issues promptly, the healthcare community has called for transformative change. A recent study by the Rwanda Biomedical Centre revealed a stark reality: only 11.5% of the population sought dental consultation within a year, while a staggering 57% had never received any form of dental care.

Oral Health Advocacy

Advocates for proactive oral healthcare have emerged, emphasizing the interconnectedness of oral health and overall physical well-being. Efforts have been made to educate the public about the importance of maintaining good oral hygiene.

Initiatives such as awareness campaigns, themed “happy mouth, happy body,” have gained traction. Collaborations with NGOs and partners have led to outreach programs aimed at empowering residents with the knowledge and resources needed to prioritize their oral hygiene.

Many have expressed their commitment to improving their dental care habits, vowing to adopt better practices and prioritize oral hygiene. As education and empowerment ripple through communities, smiles have begun to brighten, reflecting not only improved oral health but also an enhanced sense of well-being.

Through collective efforts and individual commitment, Rwanda is embarking on a journey toward a healthier, brighter future—one smile at a time. The commitment to transforming dental health is paving the way for a community that understands the importance of oral care, ultimately leading to improved quality of life and well-being for all.
